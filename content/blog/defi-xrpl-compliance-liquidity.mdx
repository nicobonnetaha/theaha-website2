## XRPL's native DeFi advantage

Most DeFi is built on top of smart contracts layered over a base chain. XRPL takes a different approach: the DEX and AMM are baked directly into the protocol.

This has profound implications for compliance, performance, and liquidity management.

## The compliance angle

XRPL's architecture allows issuers to maintain trust lines, which provides a natural enforcement point for regulatory requirements. Unlike fully permissionless DeFi, XRPL gives institutions a path to compliant decentralized trading.

This is a significant differentiator as regulators increasingly scrutinize DeFi protocols.

## Liquidity dynamics

The native AMM on XRPL launched with a novel mechanism for concentrated liquidity and fee capture. Combined with the protocol's built-in order book, it creates an interesting hybrid environment.

We've been experimenting with strategies that bridge the AMM and the order book, early results are promising.

## Challenges ahead

No ecosystem is without its challenges. XRPL's DeFi tooling is still maturing, and developer education around trust lines and payment channels remains a barrier.

We're actively working to close those gaps with better documentation and tooling. More to come.
